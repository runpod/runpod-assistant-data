# Runpod Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

Runpod has a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) that governs how the company processes customer personal data as a processor under applicable data protection laws including GDPR and US state privacy regulations.

The full DPA is available at: https://www.runpod.io/legal/data-processing-agreement

## Key Points

### Data Processing Scope

- Runpod processes customer personal data only per customer instructions as defined in the Agreement
- Processing purposes include: service delivery, customer support, and service improvement
- Runpod may create anonymized Aggregate Data without restrictions
- The DPA does not cover employee/representative data (governed by Runpod Privacy Policy)

### Security Measures

Runpod implements the following technical and organizational measures:

- Data encryption at rest and in transit via AWS/Secure Cloud
- Mandatory confidentiality agreements for all employees
- Access control policies and procedures
- Daily automated backups with monitoring
- Regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments
- Firewall and threat detection services
- ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type 2 certifications maintained

### Sub-Processors

- Runpod may engage Sub-Processors with 30-day advance notice to customers
- Customers may object to new Sub-Processors within 10 days on data protection grounds
- Runpod remains liable for Sub-Processor breaches

### Data Transfers

- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) are incorporated for EU/UK data transfers
- UK Transfer Addendum applies for UK Restricted Transfers

### Personal Data Breaches

- Runpod provides notification "without undue delay" in the event of a breach
- Customer bears sole responsibility for regulatory notifications

### Data Retention and Deletion

- Upon termination, Runpod returns or deletes all customer personal data
- Backup deletion follows scheduled routines
- Retained data (when legally required) remains confidential

### Customer Responsibilities

- Customers must manage their own account security and credentials
- A valid legal basis for processing is required throughout the term
- Prohibited data types include: SSNs, HIPAA data, payment card numbers, biometric data, and children's data

### Audits

- Customers may request audit information annually
- 30-day minimum notice required for audits
- Third-party certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001) can substitute for on-premises access

### State Privacy Laws (CCPA and Similar)

- Runpod acts as a service provider under US state privacy frameworks
- Runpod cannot sell, share, or use customer data outside specified business purposes
- Reasonable security procedures must be implemented
